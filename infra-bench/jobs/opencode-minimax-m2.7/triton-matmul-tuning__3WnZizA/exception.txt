## Tuning guidance

Use the ROCm workload-tuning guidance for Triton kernels:
`https://rocm.docs.amd.com/en/docs-7.2.4/how-to/rocm-for-ai/inference-optimization/workload.html#mi300x-autotunable-kernel-config`.

- Tune `BLOCK_SIZE_M`, `BLOCK_SIZE_N`, `BLOCK_SIZE_K`, and `GROUP_SIZE_M` to
  balance memory reuse, LDS pressure, and grid-level parallelism.
- Tune `num_warps` and `num_stages`. For a single GEMM, `num_stages=2` is a
  strong baseline; MI350X has larger LDS and may make deeper staging worth
  benchmarking.
- Include AMD-specific compile options such as `matrix_instr_nonkdim`. For this
  GEMM tutorial, `matrix_instr_nonkdim=16` is a good starting point.
- MI350X/gfx950 has more LDS per CU than MI300X, so include larger tile
  candidates such as 128x128, 256x128, or 256x256 when they fit.

The upstream tutorial already contains a reasonable HIP autotune list. You may
use that as a baseline and then benchmark alternatives.

## How you are graded

The verifier runs on fresh random FP16 matrices and checks:

1. **Config quality**: `get_hip_autotune_config()` must return multiple real
   `triton.Config` objects with varied tile sizes and AMD-specific MFMA tuning.
2. **Correctness**: `matmul(a, b)` must match `torch.matmul(a, b)` within FP16
   tolerance across several shapes.
3. **Performance gain after tuning**: your tuned kernel must be faster than the
   untuned HIP placeholder.
4. **Performance quality**: the gain must be substantial, not just measurement
   noise.
